Most Old World monkeys have tails the family name means "tailed ape", unlike the tailless apes.
The tails of Old World monkeys are not prehensile, unlike those of the New World monkeys (platyrrhines).
This just means that they cannot use thier tails as an extra limb while climbing.

Some of the sub familys

  • Baboons

    Baboons are large, social primates belonging to the genus Papio. They are native to Africa and parts of Arabia.
    Baboons are known for their strong social structures, often living in troops led by a dominant male. Characterized by their long snouts, powerful limbs, and distinctive facial features, baboons have a diet that is omnivorous. Their adaptability and intelligence allow them to navigate complex social interactions and environmental challenges.

  • Mandrills/Drills

    Mandrills and drills are two closely related species within the genus Mandrillus, both known for their striking appearance and social behavior.
    They are often mistaken for baboons those who don't realise. Found in central and west Africa.
    Mandrills are notable for their vibrant coloration, especially the males, which have blue and red facial stripes and large, colorful rumps.
    They are among the largest monkeys in the world.
    Drills resemble mandrills but have a more subdued coloration, with a less pronounced facial pattern and a more uniform body color

  • Maqacues

    Macaques are a diverse group belonging to the genus Macaca, found primarily across Asia and North Africa.
    They are adaptable, inhabiting various environments, from forests and mountains to urban areas.
    Macaques are known for their intelligence and problem-solving abilities.
    They exhibit a wide range of behaviors, from playful interactions to sophisticated social hierarchies.
    Some species, like the rhesus macaque, have adapted well to human environments, often foraging in urban areas.
