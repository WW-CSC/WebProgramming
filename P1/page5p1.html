The sub familys

  • Ponginae

    This genus has two sub species
    The bornean Orangutan Pongo pygmaeus and Sumatran Orangutan Pongo abelii.
    Orangutans have long arms, short legs, and a distinctive body shape suited for an arboreal lifestyle.
    Males can weigh between 110 to 200 pounds, while females are smaller.
    They are known for their intelligence and ability to use tools, such as using sticks to extract insects or leaves to collect water.

  • Gorilli

    This genus has two sub species the western Gorilla and the Estern Gorilla.
    Gorillas are the largest living primates, with males "silverbacks" weighing between 300 to 500 pounds.
    Gorillas inhabit tropical forests in Central and West Africa, with the Western Gorilla found in lowland rainforests and the Eastern Gorilla in mountainous regions.
    Both species are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and diseases like Ebola. Conservation efforts are crucial to their survival.

  • Panaia

    This genus has two sub species as well, being the comman chimpanze and the bonobo, which are our closest lviing ancestors
    Chimpd are found across West and Central Africa, and are known for there complex social structures and tool use.
    Bonobos aer located in the Congo Basin, they are often distinguished by their more peaceful social behavior and matriarchal societies.
    Chimpanzees are robust and have long arms, while bonobos are generally smaller and have a more slender build. Both species have large brains relative to body size.
